Gerrard: Leading Through Service

A student leader, volunteer, and learner who believes in using his strengths to support others.

About

About Me

I'm a student at St. Joseph's Institution Junior. In school, I enjoy taking on responsibilities like being a Prefect, Maths Leader, IT Leader, Prayer Leader, and a buddy to the Primary 1 students. Outside school, I spend a lot of my time volunteering, and it's something that has become really important to me because it helps me see things from other people's point of view.

Service

Serving the Community

Krsna's Free Meals

Since 2025, I've been volunteering with my mum at Krsna's Free Meals during the June and December school holidays. Three mornings a week, I reach the soup kitchen at 6.30 a.m. to help pack and serve breakfast to migrant workers, and we're usually there till about 9 a.m. The holidays are the one time I could sleep in, but my mum and I go every single time without fail, and it's become something I actually look forward to.

One morning, a worker thanked me in an Indian language I didn't understand. Even though I couldn't reply properly, I could tell he felt happy that someone cared. That moment stuck with me because it showed me that you don't need to speak the same language to make someone feel welcome.

Kampong Glam Youth Network

Since 2026, I've also been volunteering with the Kampong Glam Youth Network. So far, I've helped out at community events like Family Day, Clean & Green urban tree-planting, and children's activities. I enjoy these events because I get to meet people from different backgrounds and work together to make something fun for the community.

Leadership

Leadership in School

Combined Lasallian Schools Leadership Trip, Manila, October 2025

One of the experiences I'm most grateful for was being selected as 1 of 126 student leaders for the Combined Lasallian Schools Student Leadership Trip to Manila. It was a five-day, four-night trip that brought together prefects from SJI Junior and four other Lasallian primary schools to learn about servant leadership.

When we visited La Salle Green Hills Primary School, I got to lead a mini-group activity during a Maths lesson. I really enjoyed guiding the younger students through the task because it reminded me of being a Maths Leader back in school, where I try to explain things patiently so my classmates can follow along.

For the finale item, I helped lead the SJI Junior team by running practice sessions, encouraging my teammates, and coordinating with student leaders from the other schools. It was challenging but I learned that being a good leader means helping everyone work well together, not just doing things on your own.

One part of the trip that really stayed with me was learning about Dr Jose Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines. His story showed me what it looks like to stand up for what you believe in and serve others even when it's hard.

Growth

Growth and Resilience

Dr Jose Rizal's story also made me think about my own life. When I was younger, my mother was very unwell and my parents had to spend a lot of time in and out of hospital. I didn't fully understand what was happening at the time, but it was a period that taught me to stay strong and be grateful for the people around me.

I'm really thankful that my mum is now well and in remission. Looking back, I think that experience is part of why I care so much about helping others. The Manila trip helped me see that more clearly. It made me want to keep serving, whether as a prefect, through volunteering, or just by being there for the people around me.

Hobbies

What I Enjoy

Outside of school and volunteering, I enjoy staying active and learning new things. I train in Muay Thai and Jiu-Jitsu competitively, and I also swim regularly. These sports have taught me to push through tough training sessions and stay disciplined, even on days when I don't feel like it.

I also play the piano. I used to get really nervous performing in front of people, but I kept practising and learning to stay calm during recitals. That helped me get my ABRSM Grade 3 certificate with Merit, and I'm now working towards Grade 4.

Another thing I enjoy is playing Pokemon Go with my friends. It started as just a fun thing to do together, but I've also used it during Kampong Glam Youth Network activities to connect with younger kids and get them playing and talking to each other.

Recently, I've also started learning to vibecode, which means using AI tools to help build websites and apps. Over the past few months, I've been experimenting with tools like Cursor and Lovable, and that's actually how the idea for this portfolio site came about. I enjoy it because it lets me turn ideas into something real, even without being a professional programmer. It's exciting to see something I imagined come to life on screen.

Reflections

Looking Ahead
